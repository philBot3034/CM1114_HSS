Hall of Heroes

Here we honour the magnificent sacrifice of our brave astronauts and engeners. May their sacrifises not be in vain and our triumphs stand on their charred shoulders!

MR DEAD 1st April 2000 - 1st April 2023

Mr dead was our first and so far only test pilot. He was tasked with landing a rocket as practice for our next moon landing, this dident exactly go well. Below is footage of his final flight.


Despite all precautions, the mission ended in a spectacle that none of us could have predicted. As the engines ignited and the countdown reached its climax, there was a palpable excitement mixed with a gnawing dread among the onlookers. When the rocket finally lifted off, it seemed to defy not only gravity but the doubts of our detractors. However, the ascent was marred by an unexpected veer to the left, signaling trouble early on.


The footage, although harrowing, is an invaluable piece of history for our aerospace endeavors. As the rocket reached the edge of the atmosphere, a critical failure in the navigation system became apparent. Efforts to remotely correct the course were futile, and the rocket, stubbornly off-path, eventually succumbed to the cruel and inpridictible mistress that is physics. The descent was rapid, a fiery streak against the sky, culminating in an explosive impact that left a smoking crater as its solemn monument. The tragedy, while heartbreaking, has since propelled significant advancements in design, ensuring that the sacrifices made were not in vain.
